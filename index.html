Handshake

Effortless peer-to-peer file transfer. No password/sign-up/storage/server/bullshit required. Period.

Welcome to Handshake

Meet Handshake, a Java program that allows you to transfer files between any two Internet-connected computers without requiring a file-transfer server to act as intermediate. Light, nimble and multi-platform, Handshake is everything a good, simple file transfer program should be, without being too geeky/nerdy. And it's absolutely free-forever. Hey, you can even roll out your own version of Handshake!

Okay... why Handshake?

Works everywhere

Handshake runs on Windows, OS X, Linux....about every operating system that supports Java™. And it's really light.

Secure. Really secure.

Enterprise-grade encryption ensures that nobody snoops on your data while it is being transferred across. U mad, NSA?

Infra-free...almost

Handshake doesn't rely on servers or online services, just add internet connection. That's why we mentioned "almost".

The serious stuff now, please

Reliable encryption

Pretty Good Privacy (PGP) combined with triple-key authentication makes Handshake similar to nuts dipped in titanium. Really tough to crack.

Minimal identification

Handshake generates a unique one-word encrypted key for you that you can pass on to your partner to start business-just keep a medium to message him/her the key ready.

Lifts heavy

Whether it's 1 KB or 50 GB, Handshake doesn't care about file sizes. Whatever the size or number of files you want to transfer, you're limitless.

Isn't heavy

Really lightweight program files and installer package. You can even take it in your old PlayStation™ 2's 8 MB memory card, if conventional storage isn't your thing. Or the cloud.
